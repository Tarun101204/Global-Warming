Important International Agreements on Global Warming



The Paris Agreement (2015)

Goal: Limit global warming to well below 2°C, with efforts to restrict it to 1.5°C above pre-industrial levels.
Commitments: Countries set their own climate targets (Nationally Determined Contributions or NDCs) to reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
Monitoring: A system for tracking progress and increasing ambition over time.
Financial Support: Developed countries are encouraged to support developing nations with financial resources and technology to mitigate and adapt to climate change.
Long-Term Goal: Achieve net-zero emissions by around 2050 to stabilize the climate.

Kyoto Protocol (1997)

Goal: Legally binding targets for developed countries to reduce their greenhouse gas emissions.
Scope: Focused primarily on industrialized nations, which were historically the biggest polluters.
Mechanisms: Emissions Trading: Allows countries to buy and sell emissions allowances.
Clean Development Mechanism (CDM): Encouraged emission-reducing projects in developing countries.
Limitations: The U.S. did not ratify the treaty, and some countries failed to meet their targets, limiting its overall effectiveness.


Montreal Protocol (1987)

Goal: Phased out the use of ozone-depleting substances (ODS), such as chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs), which also have a significant impact on global warming.
Success: Widely regarded as a success in environmental diplomacy, with countries meeting their targets ahead of schedule, leading to recovery of the ozone layer.
Connection to Climate Change: Many of the substances controlled by the Montreal Protocol also contribute to global warming, so its success has indirect benefits for reducing greenhouse gases.




Key Steps for Reform and Future Action on Global Warming



Accelerating Transition to Renewable Energy


Goal: Shift from fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) to renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydro. Steps: Invest in clean energy infrastructure.
Promote energy efficiency across industries, transportation, and residential sectors.
Subsidize renewable energy technologies and phase out fossil fuel subsidies.

Carbon Pricing


Goal: Make carbon emissions more expensive to incentivize companies and individuals to reduce their carbon footprint. Steps:
Implement carbon taxes or cap-and-trade systems.
Ensure fair and equitable pricing, so that the burden does not disproportionately fall on low-income communities.

Climate Finance and Technology Transfer


Goal: Provide financial and technological support to developing countries to help them transition to cleaner economies and adapt to climate impacts.
Steps: Developed nations must fulfill their pledge to mobilize $100 billion per year in climate finance. Increase the transfer of low-carbon technologies to the Global South.

Reforestation and Carbon Sequestration


Goal: Enhance the natural capacity of ecosystems to absorb CO2.
Steps: Implement large-scale reforestation and afforestation projects. Support the development of carbon capture and storage (CCS) technologies to remove CO2 from the atmosphere.

Promoting Sustainable Agriculture


Goal: Shift towards more sustainable agricultural practices that reduce emissions, increase soil carbon storage, and improve resilience to climate impacts.
Steps: Encourage agroecology, no-till farming, crop rotation, and reduced use of synthetic fertilizers. Support local food systems and reduce food waste.


strengthening Adaptation Measures


Goal: Prepare for and mitigate the impacts of climate change, particularly in vulnerable regions.
Steps: Develop resilient infrastructure in coastal cities to protect against rising sea levels and storms. Improve early-warning systems and disaster preparedness.
Increase funding for climate adaptation projects in developing nations.
